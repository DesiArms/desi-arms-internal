How to read this

IndependentSound figure from a third-party lab (PEW Science / published shoot-off). Most trustworthy and comparable.
Mfr. claimedNumber published by the maker on their own rig. Useful, but not directly comparable across brands.
No data yetNo independent or published dB figure. Judge on design intent, not numbers.
HUBUniversal 1.375×24 rear thread. Swap any HUB mount or direct-thread adapter across rifles.
ProprietaryBrand-specific mount. Needs that brand's muzzle device on each host.
FlowFlow-through / low back pressure — less gas-to-face and easier cycling on semi-autos.

About the sound numbers: decibel and suppression figures come from different test setups, hosts, and ammunition, so treat them as directional rather than head-to-head. The most comparable data here is from PEW Science, measured 1 meter left of the muzzle on a common rifle. On supersonic .30 cal, every can on this list lands within a few decibels of the others — the real separators are back pressure, mounting, weight, and durability, not raw dB. No suppressor makes a supersonic centerfire rifle "hearing safe"; eye and ear protection is still recommended. Prices are typical retail ranges and move with rebates and availability — confirm current pricing and lead time with us.
